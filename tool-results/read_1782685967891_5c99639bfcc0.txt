   436â†’Methodology
   437â†’For each hour between 8:00 AM and 9:00 PM of each day in the analysis period (1980 to 2016), independent scores are computed for perceived temperature, cloud cover, and total precipitation. Those scores are combined into a single hourly composite score, which is then aggregated into days, averaged over all the years in the analysis period, and smoothed.
   438â†’Our cloud cover score is 10 for fully clear skies, falling linearly to 9 for mostly clear skies, and to 1 for fully overcast skies.
   439â†’Our precipitation score , which is based on the three-hour precipitation centered on the hour in question, is 10 for no precipitation, falling linearly to 9 for trace precipitation, and to 0 for 0.04 inches of precipitation or more.
   440â†’Our tourism temperature score is 0 for perceived temperatures below 50آ°F , rising linearly to 9 for 65آ°F , to 10 for 75آ°F , falling linearly to 9 for 80آ°F , and to 1 for 90آ°F or hotter.
